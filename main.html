Kashi


Varanasi is perceived as site of ‘vigor and rigor’ where religious culture, people and society get interconnected deeply as mosaic called “microcosmic India”, or the ‘Cultural Capital of India’ (see Fig. 1.1). The city’s population (1.45 million in 2011; and estimated over 2.57 million including surrounding peri-urban areas in 2020) consists of Hindus (63%), Muslim (32%) and other religious groups.
